History

Monopoly, a popular board game sold in over 103 countries, originated in the United States in 1903. Initially known as "The Landlord's Game," it was created by Elizabeth Magie to illustrate the negative aspects of concentrating land in private monopolies. The game was modified over time and was eventually rebranded as Monopoly by Charles Darrow in the 1930s, who sold it to Parker Brothers. Despite the controversy over its origin, Monopoly went on to become a phenomenal success, with numerous variations created over the years, including city-themed, sports-themed, movie-themed editions, and even video game versions. It continues to be a global favorite in board games.

Rules of game

  • Start with $1500.
  • Roll dice to move.
  • Buy or auction unowned properties.
  • Pay rent for owned properties.
  • Follow instructions on Chance or Community Chest cards.
  • Pay tax on Tax spaces.
  • Buy unowned Railroads or Utilities, or pay rent if owned by others.
  • No gain/loss on Free Parking.
  • Go to Jail for infractions. Pay $50, use card, or roll doubles to get out.
  • Buy houses/hotels when owning all properties of the same color.
  • Bankruptcy occurs if you can't pay a debt.
  • Last player standing wins.

Tips and Tricks

  • Buy railroads.
  • Buy utilities.
  • Buy red and orange properties.
  • Three houses are better than one.
  • Monopoly money isn't everything.
  • Create a housing shortage
  • Statistics are your friend.
  • Going to jail can be a good thing
